Hey all! Thanks for taking the time to check out our "Garrison Happenings" newsletter.  These probably won't follow a specific release schedule or anything, but we figured it would be a cool way to give a shout out to some new recruits and approvals, highlight an event or two, let people know about any new merchandise they might want to get in on, and some other fun stuff!

We're nearing the end of June already, and it feels like the year is flying by!  ComiConn is less than a week away, and our signup list is huge this year. 
This year we accomplished a lot so far: a complete overhaul of our forums; a new methodology for signups and tracking events; a new shirt run; armor parties that have had more recruits than I ever imagined for our small state garrison; and new lines of communications with our neighbors to the north, east, and west.  This has all come with a ton of help from a lot of people, and I can't even begin to thank everyone who has helped make this year as amazing as it has been already enough!

So what's next you might be asking?

This weekend we'll be doing Blast-A-Trooper at ComiConn, in support NAMI. 
What's Blast-A-Trooper?  The quick explanation is folks donation in exchange for NERF darts to shoot at our troopers.  It's a lot of fun, makes for some silly pictures and memories, and puts a lot of smiles on faces.   If you've never participated in one I highly recommend coming down to ComiConn this weekend and checking it out.

In July we'll be rolling out our new TroopTracker page, which will be integrated directly into the CTG website.  Members will be able to see a tally of events they've participated in, as well as monthly, yearly, and all time standings across the Garrison.  This will integrate into our forums with special badges for achieving participation goals.  There's even exclusive merchandise in the works to show off your tour of duty for the Emperor!

The next couple of events in the coming months include TV appearances, parades, conventions, and so much more!  Stay tuned

~ Sarah Bailey

I also wanted to congratulate all of our new troopers that have made it through their builds and the approval process.  Way to go on finishing your costumes and I look forward to trooping with you all soon.

We sat down with Nick Scofield (TX-686) this edition to figure out what makes him tick and if there is anything we could to do stay on his "do not kill" list.

Q: What made you decide to get into costuming? How did you learn about the 501st?
A: Well now that’s a good question! It all started back in 2000 when Jesus was born! It wasn’t costuming per say that I wanted to get into. I was perusing the interspace on day as a young lad and found some pics of people who had built Stormtrooper armor. Being a fanboy of Star Wars my entire life, I knew immediately that I wanted one! I HAD to be a stormtrooper! So started my quest and that all led me to the 501st. I believe I found the 501st while looking for armor and had to be a part of it. Purchased my first TK Armor, an FX kit (the standard back then but horribly inaccurate) and built it at my grandma’s house. Submitted the pics and it was all downhill from there!

Cool story bro
Q: What was your first troop you did with the group? What were some first impressions?

A: aahhh...first troop! It’s like a first date! But sweatier! Mine was at a place called Camp Coniston in New Hampshire. They had a Star Wars themed day of activities for the kids and we set it up so we walked in on them during camp breakfast and kidnapped one of the counselors dressed as Leia. Vader made the kids do challenges / activities all day to win here back. We stayed the night as a group in a private cabin and had a great time. The camp was amazing to us and all of the group was awesome to me. To this day it was one of the best troops I’ve ever done.

Q: Who is your favorite Current Executive Officer in the CTG?
A: why Joe Civitello of course!

Q:  What would you like to see out of the CTG or 501st in the near future?

A: I think I would like to see more social gatherings like monthly meetings or armor parties, as well as a pillow fightd

Q: How'd you like Solo?
A: I thought it was just good the first time and was on the fence of it being great but saw it a second time last night and enjoyed it much more.  Better than TLJ though, but not Rogue one

 
Q: Other than being an amazing Deathtrooper distributing peace and prosperity with extreme prejudice throughout the empire, what do you like to do for fun?
A: Skateboard, Play Call of Duty <Redacted> and <Redacted>... and Knit
 
Q: You Knit?
A: Only when spending the night in jail
 
OK I think that's where we're going to stop this interview!
Thanks for participating Nick... I guess...
 
Stay tuned for future interviews with hopefully less terrifying CTG Members!
 
~ Joe Civitello
